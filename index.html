Biography

I grew up in Slovakia, studying at the local elementary school and high school in my hometown. I took up an interest in Mathematics, Computer science and Physics early on, participating at competitions such as the Mathematical Olympiad and Physics Olympiad and also writing some exploratory papers on some interesting mathematical topics starting at the end of elementary school, some of which are posted below. After high school, I decided to enroll at the University of Bocconi to attend the Mathematics for AI program. During my years in Milan I further developed my mathematical skills as well as my coding abilities. After finishing my degree there, I decided to enroll at the University of Vienna for the Masters in Mathematics program. Currently, I am also working as a student research assistant at the University of Vienna.

Papers

Introduction to Irrational Numbers (Unpublished)

This was the first paper I have ever written, I was 15 years old at the time. As one can imagine, there is nothing groundbreaking about this paper, but it provides a solid foundation in irrational numbers for those interested with some prerequisite mathematical background.

Intuitive but rarely proven Theorems (Unpublished)

This is a fun paper I wrote just to collect some neat ideas I had throughout highschool. The theorems are in no way connected to one another but rather are supposed to be often overlooked in each respective area, while still being used a lot or having great applications.

Intuitive but rarely proven Theorems

On the Sums of Powers of Natural Numbers (Unpublished)

This was a huge leap for me in terms of my mathematical achievements. I derived a closed formula (albeit not very useful) for a notorious sum which was solved first time by Jacob Bernoulli. I used a very different and new approach to derive the formula.
